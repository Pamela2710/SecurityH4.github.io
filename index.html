Design

In your design, you must provide a list of the data that you need to collect for the operation of the application. For each, provide a description of why you need this data. Examples:


  • Who are the users of your application? What kind of information do you need of each of them?

    • The application is aimed at people with safe transportation needs, who have access to digital media and an internet connection. On the other hand, the application is also aimed at people with access to a means of transportation who are looking for flexible opportunities to generate income from their transportation service.

  • What data do you need to make the user experience better? Examples: better ride experience, shorter wait time, faster routes, etc

    • Historical ride data as well as user behaviour data could be of great use to predict, based on time of day and location, where a user might be headed. This could enhance the user experience by suggesting the more likely destinations a rider might be in need of.


    Demand heat maps would greatly benefit the drivers by providing an overlook of places that have a high demand of rides based on time of the day. This would allow for drivers to supply said demand properly rather than staying on an area of relatively low activity. Likewise, earnings data could provide drivers with an accurate summary of their earnings based on location, time, etc., allowing them to plan their day strategically.


    Finally, real-time traffic data, and route optimization data would allow for a better optimization of the ride, meaning shorter wait times, less time in traffic, and reducing time costs which is beneficial to both the rider and the driver

  • What kind of data do you need to collect to measure the performance and efficiency of the application?

    • Measuring performance and efficiency could be done by observing some key indicators. It's important to have a responsive applications, users would stop using it if it had long load times or if it crashed often. Travel and wait time as well as distance traveled as an indicator of route selection algorithms is valuable as well.


    Tracking of cancellation reasons could be a key indicator of why people are choosing to not use the service and might uncover potential areas of improvement. Which coupled with usage frequency, retention rates, and peak usage times could help improve user engagement metrics.

  • What about payment and tipping?

    • Handling of payment, be it billing or tipping, requires the implementation of secure payment services. Strong security measures are usually bundled within the chosen services and are crucial when keeping financial information safe. This information tends to be credit card details, billing addresses, trip and tip information such as cost, fees, and any extra charges.

Privacy Design

Include a privacy design for the application.

  • For all the data that is being collected, what can be seen and by whom?
    • Type of user Visible Data
    Passengers
    • Own profile and preferences.
    • Personal travel history.
    • Grades given.
    • Basic information about the driver assigned to the current trip: name, photo and qualifications.
    Drivers
    • Own profile and statistics.
    • History of trips made.
    • Grades received.
    • Current passenger data: location, name, preferences.
    Administrators
    • Complete profiles of users and drivers.
    • Financial information.
    • Performance and efficiency reports.
    • Operational data to manage the platform.
    Software developers
    • Technical data, error logs, performance reports.
    • Data related to the technical infrastructure and maintenance of the application.
    Data Analysts
    • Detailed reporting and analysis on application performance and efficiency.
    Executives
    • Financial analysis for decision-making.
  • What information should have restricted access?

    • Passenger information such as a contact number and starting and destination addresses should be available only to the rider, and the driver only when there is an ongoing trip. Other types of personal data such as login credentials, feedback, and payment information should only be available to the user who owns them.


    Likewise, driver sensitive data like driver's license and earnings should only be freely available to the driver they belong to. Similarly, pickup location and destination of a user should only be seen by the driver when there's an ongoing trip.


    Some of this data, like route and time taken, cost, and payment method, only partially, should only be seen by support personnel when requested by a driver or rider. For example, when dealing with a support ticket submitted by a user.

  • What information should not be seen by anyone?

    • Financial information is a critical point here, full details of credit cards and/or accounts should only be seen by secure transaction systems. Likewise, login credentials for any user should only be handled through automated systems and never accessible to any type of user. Full location and communication history should be only available to riders and drivers, protected with end-to-end encryption.

Pen-tester

Assume you are a pen-tester and you are in charge of testing the privacy design proposed.


  • What are the risks if you are able to get access to the app as a user, as an admin, and as developer?

    • As a user, frequently visited addresses, payment information, and contact information.


    As an admin, this could mean access to complete user profiles, financial information, permissions to change platform configurations, and disruption of service availability.


    As a developer, this would enable the tester to access API keys and server credentials, potentially granting the ability to introduce vulnerabilities or backdoors.

  • In any of these cases, what are the ways that an attacker can provoke a privacy breach? What kind of attacks can be used to accomplish this.

    • To users, the attacker can perform phishing attacks with malicious websites, testing with databases of stolen credentials, or attempting to steal session tokens.


    To admins, it's the same case as users, but adding social engineering to obtain administrator's credentials, or bribing one into misusing their access.


    To developers, the attacker could try to exploit insecure developer tools or endpoints, such as code injection, or looking for misconfigured access controls and permissions.

  • Write an ethical analysis of the consequences of a privacy breach of the app for each of the cases above.

    • To users, this could represent a threat to personal safety if their trip data is leaked. Likewise, it could result in identity theft and/or financial loss for the affected user.


    To admins, a breach could represent widespread identity theft and fraud. Which in turn would cause the people in charge of the app to face regulatory fines and legal action.


    To developers, the breach would see the possibility of systematic failure and widespread unauthorized data access. This could be used to launch further attacks on users, cascading into the issues described for users and admins. A vulnerability of this magnitude would have severe legal and financial repercussions.
